Unveiling Excellence: Meet Our Event Main Committee

(First Committee)


Disarmament and International Security aim to reduce and eliminate weapons to ensure global peace and stability. This involves nuclear disarmament, arms control, and preventing the spread of weapons of mass destruction, facilitated by international treaties and organizations like the United Nations.

Disarmament and International Security

(Second Committee)


The Economic and Financial Committee focuses on global economic and financial issues, including sustainable development, international trade, and financial stability. It works to promote economic growth, reduce poverty, and address global financial crises through policy recommendations and international cooperation.

Economic and Financial Committee

(Third Committee)


This area addresses global social challenges, human rights, and cultural preservation. It focuses on improving social development, protecting human rights, promoting gender equality, and safeguarding cultural heritage. Efforts include advancing education, health, and welfare, combating discrimination, and supporting humanitarian aid.

Social, Humanitarian & Cultural Issues

(Fourth Committee)


The Special Political and Decolonization Committee addresses issues related to decolonization, peacekeeping, and political stability. It works on resolving conflicts, supporting self-determination for non-self-governing territories, and overseeing peacekeeping missions. The committee also deals with topics such as mine action, outer space, and the impact of atomic radiation.

Special Political and Decolonization

(Fifth Committee)


The Administrative and Budgetary Committee oversees the financial and administrative aspects of United Nations operations. It reviews and approves the organization's budget, ensures efficient use of resources, and monitors financial accountability. The committee plays a critical role in promoting transparency and effective management within the UN system.

Administrative and Budgetary Committee
